1. MENORRHAGIA (HEAVY MENSTRUAL BLEEDING) :

It refers to the condition of heavy and prolonged vaginal bleeding with menstrual cycle. Heavy periods aren't usually life-threatening, but they can be if you loose too much blood. Usually a woman typically loses about 25-50 ml of blood in her single menstrual cycle. Little heavy flow during periods is normal and does not need sudden attention,but one needs to be careful if any of the following symptoms are occuring.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Needing to change a pad or tampon every hour or less in a row
- Needing to use two pads or other types of double protection at a time
- Needing to change a pad or tampon even during the night
- Passing blood clots larger than a quarter
- Looking pale
- A period that lasts longer than a week
- Feeling tired, dizzy or shortness of breath after bleeding

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Hormonal problems includes imbalance of hormones like estrogen and progesterone
- Uterine issues like fibroid growth and polyps
- Bleeding disorder conditions including von Willebrand disease
- Few STI's like chlamydia, gonorrhea and trichomoniasis
- Usage of few medications like anti-depressants
- Pregnancy complications like miscarriage
- Thyroid issues, kidney or liver issues

~ HOME REMEDIES
- Drink water as much as possible
- Eat more foods rich in Vitamin C and Iron
- Also consume foods rich in omega-3 oils, like fish, nuts and seeds
- Take herbs like ginger, turmeric, raspberry leaf
- Try drinking a mixture of boiled coriander seeds and cinnamon
- Try applying a heat pad, use a menstrual cup
- Avoid intake of caffeine

~ STEPS TO TAKE IN EMERGENCY SITUATIONS
- Stay calm and call emergency services if bleeding is severe
- Have the person lie down with their feet elevated about 12 inches
- Use a clean cloth to apply firm, steady pressure to vaginal area
- Place a cushion under hips to help reduce bleeding
- Apply a cold compress or an ice pack wrapped in a cloth to the lower abdomen to help constrict blood vessels
- Keep track of amount and frequency of bleeding

~ WHEN TO SEEK A DOCTOR
- Losing consciousness unexpectedly
- Passing larger clots repeatedly
- Experiencing shortness of breath or chest pain

Doctor may perform a vaginal exam to check the cervix and size and shape of the uterus and make sure to not worry and stay calm and positive.



2. HYPOMENORRHEA (LIGHT MENSTRUAL BLEEDING) :

Also known as short or scanty periods. In this a female has extremely light menstrual blood flow. Although, in few females it may be normal to have less bleeding as less blood flow may be genetic. However, one needs to pay attention if you experience a consistent shift in your cycle and if needed should consult a doctor.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Bleeding less than normal for two or more menstrual cycles consecutively
- Periods that don't last longer than two days
- Very light bleeding
- More frequent light periods than the typical 21- to -35 day cycle
- Reduction in the need to change pads or tampons

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Frequent stress, anxiety and depression
- Genetics can be a factor
- Doing excessive exercise and lifting heavy weights
- Eating disorders such as Anorexia, Nervosa and Bulimia
- Performing hormonal birth control methods like consuming birth control pills or IUDs
- Low body fat and crash dieting
- Breastfeeding
- Other causes include PCOS, Hypothyroidism

~ HOME REMEDIES
- Stay hydrated and increase Iron intake
- Do warm compresses by soaking a cloth in warm water and then apply it to lower abdomen
- Drink aloe vera juice
- Consume Cumin seeds (Ajwain) after soaking them overnight
- Consume Turmeric milk or Turmeric tea
- Drink Ginger tea
- Perform yoga

~ WHEN TO SEEK A DOCTOR
- Miss three or more periods in a row
- Have very irregular periods
- Have other symptoms like weight gain, tiredness, hair growth on your face
- Are struggling to get pregnant


A light period is usually not a cause for concern, as it can be a normal fluctuations in your menstrual cycle. Just little changes in your lifestyle can help in improving your conditions.



3. AMENORRHEA (NO MENSTRUAL BLEEDING) :

It is defined as the absence of menstruation, often defined as missing one or more menstrual periods. Primary amenorrhea refers to the absence of menstruation in someone who has not had a period by age 15. It can be a natural part of life, such as during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Sometimes, females may skip their menstrual cycles especially when they have just started their menstruations. However, it's still essential to pay attention to few symptoms and consult a doctor if needed.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Absence of menstrual periods for more than 3 cycles
- Excess body hair
- Headache and vision changes
- Weight gain or weight loss
- Vaginal dryness
- Milky discharge from the breasts
- Lack of breast development

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Excessive exercise, low body weight
- Poor nutrition, Eating disorder
- Emotional stress, anxiety, depression
- Hormonal issues like thyroid disorders, pituitary gland issues or hormone disruption
- Usage of few medications like antipsychotics, antidepressants, blood pressure drugs
- Pregnancy, breastfeeding, birth control pills, Intrauterine devices(IUDs)
- Chronic illnesses, diabetes, cystic fibrosis, PCOS

~ HOME REMEDIES
- Stay hydrated and perform yoga
- Eat more foods rich in Vitamin C and Iron
- Also consume foods rich in omega-3 oils, like fish, nuts and seeds
- Take herbs like ginger, turmeric, red clover
- Try drinking a mixture of boiled coriander seeds and cinnamon
- Manage stress and get enough sleep
- Apply castor oil and massage it to low abdomen area

~ WHEN TO SEEK A DOCTOR
- Miss three or more menstrual periods
- Are aged 15 or older and have never had a menstrual period
- Have had fewer than 9 periods in an year
- Are a teenager and have no sign of breast development by age 13



4. IRREGULAR MENSTRUAL BLEEDING :

Irregular mentrual bleeding is mainly categorized in three ways :-
a.METRORRHAGIA - Refers to irregular uterine bleeding that occurs at irregular intervals, often between periods. This bleeding may be HEAVY OR LIGHT and can occur at any time without any regular pattern.
b.MENOMETRORRHAGIA - A type of irregular bleeding characterized by HEAVY AND PROLONGED bleeding often accompanied by bleeding between periods. This bleeding may be extremely heavy often required to change sanitary products.
c.POLYMENORRHEA - It refers to frequent menstrual bleeding, occuring at intervals of less than 21 days. Here, menstrual cycles are shorter than usual, resulting in more frequent periods.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Heavy bleeding and severe pain
- Prolonged bleeding that lasts more than 7 days
- Bleeding at irregular intervals
- Fatigue, weakness or shortness of breath
- Frequent menstrual bleeding
- Menstrual cycles shorter than 21 days

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Hormonal problems includes imbalance of hormones like estrogen and progesterone
- Uterine issues like fibroid growth and polyps
- Frequent stress, anxiety and depression
- Adenomyosis (where tissue similar to the lining of uterus grows into the muscular wall of the uterus)
- Pituitary gland problems
- Thyroid disorders

~ HOME REMEDIES
- Drink water as much as possible
- Eat more foods rich in Vitamin C and Iron
- Perform warm compresses to lower abdomen area
- Take herbs like ginger, turmeric, raspberry leaf
- Perform relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga
- Get plenty of rest

~ WHEN TO SEEK A DOCTOR
- Heavy bleeding which is consistent and is not normal
- Having severe pain in pelvic area accompanied by vomiting
- Experiencing frequent and consistent dizziness or lightheadedness
- Sudden high fever which is persistent

Remember sometimes these symptoms can be normal for some females hence it's important to stay calm and seek expert advice only if the symptoms are persistent with less or no improvement.



5. SPOTTING (VERY LIGHT BLOOD SPOTS) :

It is a type of vaginal bleeding that occurs outside of a woman's regular menstrual period. It's typically lighter than a regular period, with only small amount of blood or discharge.It can last long for some 2-3 days or even more than week in few cases. Sometimes its normal to experience this type of flow, however, one needs to pay attention to its persistent symptoms and when to seek medical help.


~ SYMPTOMS
- It can be in pink, brown or red color discharge
- Not requiring pad or tampon
- Happening anytime during menstrual cycle including before, during, after your period
- Abdominal pain, change in vaginal discharge
- Some serious symptoms including pain during urination or intercourse

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Hormonal fluctuations
- Physical or emotional stress, anxiety, depression
- Birth control pills
- Pregnancy
- Uterine issues like fibroid growth and polyps
- Infections like yeast infection or other infections
- Thyroid disorders

~ HOME REMEDIES
- Stay hydrated
- Eat more foods rich in Vitamin C and Iron
- Also consume foods rich in omega-3 oils, like fish, nuts and seeds
- Take herbs like ginger, turmeric, cummin seeds
- Apply a warm compress to the lower abdomen area
- Take proper rest

~ WHEN TO SEEK A DOCTOR
- If spotting becomes heavy bleeding
- Experiencing severe pain
- Consistent dizziness or lightheadedness
- High fever which is persistent

Remember that spotting can be a normal experience for some women and one should not get panic immediately.
