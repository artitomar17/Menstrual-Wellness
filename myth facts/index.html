Menstrual Myths vs. Facts

Myth: You can't get pregnant during your period.
Fact: It's less likely, but pregnancy can still occur during menstruation.
Myth: Period blood is dirty.
Fact: Menstrual blood is a natural bodily fluid and not inherently dirty.
Myth: Periods are embarrassing and should be hidden.
Fact: Menstruation is a natural, healthy part of woman's body. There's no reason to be embarrassed or to hide it.
Myth: Only women gets periods.
Fact: Transgender men and nonbinary people may get their periods. Periods are not always just a "woman's" issue, it's a human issue.
Myth: You shouldn't exercise during your period.
Fact: Exercise can alleviate menstrual cramps and improve mood.
Myth: PMS isn't real; women make it up.
Fact: PMS is a legitimate medical condition affecting many women.
Myth: You shouldn't swim during your period.
Fact: Swimming is safe during menstruation with appropriate products.
Myth: Menstrual cycles sync among women living together.
Fact: No scientific evidence supports menstrual synchrony.
Myth: Tampons or cups can take away your virginity.
Fact: Virginity is a concept; these products do not affect it.
Myth: You shouldn't bathe during your period.
Fact: Bathing is hygienic and can relieve discomfort during periods.
Myth: Drinking cold beverages affects menstruation.
Fact: No scientific link exists between cold drinks and menstruation.
Myth: Period pain is normal and should be endured.
Fact: Severe pain might signal issues and deserves medical attention.
