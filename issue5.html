HORMONAL ISSUES



A hormonal disorder occurs when there's an imbalance of hormones in the body, disrupting various bodily functions. Hormones, produced by endocrine glands, regulate growth, metabolism, reproduction, and mood. When hormone levels are abnormal, it can lead to issues like irregular periods, infertility, weight gain or loss, mood swings, and fatigue. Common hormonal disorders include polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), thyroid disorders, Cushing's syndrome, and diabetes. Below are the few complications which we will talk about.

1. PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) :

It is a hormonal disorder which affects women of reproductive age. It's a complex condition that affects 5-10% of women of childbearing age, making it one of the most common endocrine disorders among women. It can lead to certain more complications like miscarriage, cardiovascular diseases, diabetes. Hence, it's important to consider its symptoms and seek medical advice at the earliest note.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Menstrual irrergularities, infrequent or prolonged periods
- Weight gain which is unusual
- Excessive hair growth on face, chest, back
- Getting acne, oily skin and dandruff
- Thinning hair on the scalp
- Cysts on the ovaries detected by ultrasound
- Infertility

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Hormonal imbalance including high levels of androgens and insulin resistance
- Genetics, which tends to run in families
- Insulin resistance, leading to high blood sugar levels
- Excess weight or obesity
- Exposure to environment toxins such as pesticides and heavy metals

It's diagnosis can include the process of certain physical and pelvic exams, medical history checks and ultrasound. And for treatment certain medications and few lifestyle changes are required as prescribes by your doctor.



2. Thyroid Disorder :

Thyroid disorder occurs when the thyroid gland doesn't produce the right amount of thyroid hormones. The THYROID GLAND is a small, butterfly-shaped gland located in the neck, just above the collar bone. It plays a vital role in regulating metabolism, growth, and development. The two main types of thyroid disorders are :-
1. HYPOTHYROIDISM (Underactive Thyroid) - Here, The thyroid gland doesn't produce the right amount of thyroid hormones, leading to a slower metabolism.
2. HYPERTHYROIDISM (Overactive Thyroid) - Here, the thyroid gland produces too many thyroid hormones, leading to faster metabolism.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Irregular periods
- Heavy or light bleeding
- Infertility or recurrent miscarriages
- Unexplained weight gain or loss
- Hair loss or brittle hair
- Persistent fatigue or lethargic
- Sensitive to cold temperature

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Iodine deficiency
- Thyroid surgery
- Pituitary gland problem
- Certain medications
- Autoimmune Thyroiditis, where the immune system attacks thyroid gland

It's diagnosis can include the process of thyroid function tests (TFTs), physical exams and medical history. And for treatment certain medications and few lifestyle changes are required. In few cases, surgeries can be needed as well. as prescribes by your doctor.



3. Cushing's Syndrome :

Cushing's syndrome is a rare endocrine disorder caused by excess levels of cortisol in the body. Cortisol is a hormone produced by the adrenal gland that plays a crucial role in response to stress, regulating blood sugar, and aiding in metabolism. Cushing's syndrome can disrupt menstrual cycles due to the excess cortisol levels, which can:
1. Suppress Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone (GnRH) : regulates the release of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH), essential for ovulation and menstrual cycles.
2.Inhibit Estrogen Production : Cortisol can suppress estrogen production, leading to irregular menstrual cycles or amenorrhea.
3. Disrupt Ovulation : Cushing's syndrome can disrupt ovulation, leading to irregular menstrual cycles or infertility.


~ SYMPTOMS
- Irregular periods
- Fat accumulation at the back of neck
- Rounded face due to fat accumulation
- Purple strech marks on the abdomen, thigh
- Excessive hair growth on face, chest, back
- Severe acne on face, chest
- Central obesity, particularly in the abdomen area

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- High stress level which is unmanageable
- A tumor in the adrenal gland that produces excess cortisol
- A non-cancerous tumor in the pituitary gland that produces excess adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH), stimulating the adrenal gland to produce cortisol
- Prolonged use of corticosteroid medications, such as prednisone, can cause Cushing's syndrome
- Familial Cushing's Syndrome, a rare genetic disorder that affects the adrenal gland's ability to regulate cortisol production
- Ectopic ACTH-producing Tumor, a rare tumor outside the pituitary gland that produces excess ACTH

It's diagnosis can include the process of physical exams, medical history, laboratory and imaging tests. And it's treatment depends on the underlying cause and may include medications, surgeries, radiation and hormone replacement therapy.



4. POF (Premature Ovarian Failure) :

It is also known as premature ovarian insufficiency (POI) and it affects women under the age of 40. It is a condition where ovaries stop functioning and hormone level decreases leading to irregular or absent menstrual cycles.

~ SYMPTOMS
- Irregular periods
- Night sweats
- Hot flashes can occur due to decrease in estrogen levels
- Vaginal dryness and painful sex
- Sleep disturbances such as insomnia

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Genetic disorders such as Turner syndrome
- Autoimmune disorder, which can cause the immune system to attack the ovaries
- Pelvic infections can damage the ovaries
- Surgical removal of ovaries or damage to ovaries during surgery
- Chemotherapy and radiations
- Exposure to certain environmental toxins such as pesticides

It's diagnosis can include the process of physical exams, medical history, pelvic examination and hormone level tests. And it's treatment may include birth control pills, hormone replacement therapy, fertility medications and few lifestyle changes.



5. Hyperprolactinemia :

Hyperprolactinemia is a hormonal disorder characterized by an excessive production of prolactin, a hormone produced by the pituitary gland. In females, hyperprolactinemia can disrupt menstrual cycles and cause a range of symptoms.

~ SYMPTOMS
- Irregular periods
- Galactorrhea, which is spontaneous milk production or leakage from the breasts, which can be embarrassing and uncomfortable.
- Infertility
- Headaches or migraines
- Weight gain and acne problem which is unusual

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Pituitary adenoma, A non-cancerous tumor in the pituitary gland that produces excess prolactin
- Hypothyroidism, An underactive thyroid gland can stimulate the production of prolactin
- Certain medications, such as antidepressants, antipsychotics, and blood pressure medications
- Physical and emotional stress
- Sleep apnea, insomnia, or other sleep disorders

It's diagnosis can include the process of few blood tests, imaging tests. And it's treatment may include hormone replacement therapy and few lifestyle changes like exercise, healthy diet, adequate sleep etc.



6. Adrenal Disorders :

Adrenal disorders refer to conditions that affect the adrenal glands, which are two small glands located on top of the kidneys. These glands play a crucial role in producing hormones that regulate various bodily functions.

~ SYMPTOMS
- Mood changes, carried out by anxiety, depression
- Feeling tired or weak
- Blood pressure changes
- Hormonal and electrolyte imbalances
- Weight changes and skin changes

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Genetic mutations, some adrenal disorders are caused by genetic mutations that affect the production of adrenal hormones
- Tumors, benign or malignant tumors can develop on the adrenal glands, leading to an overproduction or underproduction of adrenal hormones
- Infections, certain infections, such as tuberculosis, can damage the adrenal glands and lead to adrenal insufficiency
- Autoimmune disorders, certain autoimmune disorders, such as Addison's disease, can cause the immune system to attack the adrenal glands

It's diagnosis can include the process of few blood tests, imaging tests. And it's treatment may include hormone replacement therapy and few lifestyle changes like exercise, healthy diet, adequate sleep etc.



7. Pituitary Gland Disorders :

The pituitary gland is a small, pea-sized gland located at the base of the brain. It plays a crucial role in regulating various bodily functions. Pituitary gland disorders occur when the gland doesn't function properly, leading to hormonal imbalances.

~ SYMPTOMS
- Hormonal imbalances
- Fatigue and feeling weak
- Vision problems
- Infertility

~ POSSIBLE CAUSES
- Tumors, benign or malignant tumors can develop in the pituitary gland
- Inflammation in the pituitary gland
- Certain infections, such as meningitis or tuberculosis
- Genetic disorders
- Head trauma

It's diagnosis can include the process of few blood tests, imaging tests and stimulation tests. And it's treatment may include few medications, radiation therapy and few lifestyle changes like exercise, healthy diet, adequate sleep etc.
